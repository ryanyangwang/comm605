Course overview

This is the tutorial website for COMM 605 Social media analytics and research at Rochester Institute of Technology. All the codes in the tutorials are created by the instructor and open access resources, including but not limited to:

  • Wickham, H., √áetinkaya-Rundel, M., & Grolemund, G. (2023). R for data science (2nd version).
  • Liang, H. COMM3650: Social Media Analytics for Communication Professionals.

This course focuses on social media research with various methodological approaches to study public data, users and messages. Students will be introduced to a variety of techniques and concepts used to obtain, monitor and evaluate social media content with a focus on how the analytics could inform communication strategies. During the course, students will also learn how to design and evaluate social media-based research studies.

Objectives

Students who successfully complete assigned coursework should develop the following skills:

  • explain the theoretical and methodological perspectives that guide social media research;
  • apply computational tools and practice basic data science skills to answer empirical research questions and conduct social science research;
  • identify major milestones in social media platforms and research;
  • critique on how algorithms and (social media)data may affect civil societies in both positive and adverse ways.
